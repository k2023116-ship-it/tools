How to Use the Color Picker Tool

Extract beautiful colors from any image and create stunning color palettes and gradients with our easy-to-use tool.

1

Upload Your Image

Click the "Upload Image" button or simply drag and drop your image file into the designated area. We support all common image formats including JPG, PNG, GIF, and WebP.

2

Pick Colors

Once your image is loaded, click anywhere on the image to extract the color from that specific pixel. The color will appear in the swatch along with its HEX and RGB values.

3

Build Your Palette

Click the "+ Add" button to add the selected color to your palette. Repeat this process to collect multiple colors from your image and build a comprehensive color scheme.

4

Generate Gradients

Once you have at least 2 colors in your palette, click "Generate" to create a beautiful gradient using your selected colors. Perfect for backgrounds and design elements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What image formats are supported?
A: Our tool supports all common image formats including JPG, JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP, and BMP files.
Q: Is there a file size limit for images?
A: While there's no strict limit, we recommend keeping images under 10MB for optimal performance. Larger files may take longer to process.
Q: Can I save my color palettes?
A: Currently, palettes are stored in your browser session. You can copy the HEX/RGB values to save them externally. We're working on export features for future updates.
Q: How accurate are the color values?
A: Color values are extracted directly from image pixels and are highly accurate. However, monitor calibration and display settings can affect how colors appear visually.
Q: Does this work on mobile devices?
A: Yes! Our color picker is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. You can upload photos directly from your mobile device.
Q: Are my images stored on your servers?
A: No, your privacy is important to us. All image processing happens locally in your browser. Images are never uploaded to our servers.
Q: Can I extract colors from screenshots?
A: Absolutely! Screenshots work perfectly with our tool. This is great for analyzing website designs, app interfaces, and digital artwork.
Q: How do I copy color codes?
A: Click on any color in your palette or the main swatch area, then select and copy the HEX or RGB values displayed in the color information panel.
Q: Can I remove colors from my palette?
A: Yes! Click the "√ó" button next to any color in your palette to remove it. This helps you refine your color selection and keep only the colors you need.
Q: What's the difference between HEX and RGB?
A: HEX codes (like #FF5733) are commonly used in web design and CSS, while RGB values (like rgb(255, 87, 51)) are used in design software and represent Red, Green, and Blue color channels.
